# PM Interview Frameworks — Quick Reference Card

Based on *Cracking the PM Interview*, Chapter 15 and throughout.

---

## Product Design: 7-Step Framework (Ch 14)
For "Design a product" questions.

1. Ask clarifying questions — understand scope & context
2. Provide a structure — tell the interviewer your approach upfront
3. Identify users and customers — who are all the people involved?
4. What are use cases and goals? — what are they trying to accomplish?
5. How well does the current product serve those use cases? — where are the gaps?
6. What features/changes would improve the weak spots? — tie to user needs
7. Wrap up — summarize your solution

**Anti-pattern:** Jumping to features without going through users first.

---

## Product Improvement: Adapted Design Framework (Ch 14)
For "How would you improve X?" questions.

1. Clarify the goal of the existing product
2. Identify target users and their key use cases
3. Assess current product against those use cases — where does it fall short?
4. Propose improvements tied directly to the identified gaps
5. Define metrics that would validate the improvement worked

---

## Favorite Product Framework (Ch 14)
For "What's your favorite product and why?" questions.

1. Product goal — what is it trying to accomplish?
2. Target users — who uses it?
3. Key metrics — where does it excel? Where does it struggle?
4. What you love — specific UX or product decisions (with reason)
5. What you'd change — backed by a user pain point, with metric validation plan

---

## Estimation: 8-Step Framework (Ch 13)

1. Clarify the question — what exactly are we measuring?
2. Catalog what you know (or wish you knew)
3. Make an equation — write the formula before numbers
4. Think about edge cases and alternate sources
5. Break it down — solve each component separately
6. Review & state assumptions — explain your reasoning
7. Do the math — round aggressively, label units
8. Sanity check — does the answer make intuitive sense?

**Math tricks:**
- Rule of 72: years to double = 72 ÷ growth rate %
- Orders of magnitude: digits(a) + digits(b) = digits(a×b) ± 1

## STAR Method — Behavioral Questions (Ch 12)

- **S**ituation: Brief context (where, when, what was happening)
- **T**ask: Your specific responsibility
- **A**ction: What *you* did (first person, specific)
- **R**esult: Quantified outcome + what you learned

**Rule:** "We" kills your story. Use "I."

---

## Customer Purchase Decision: AIDA / REAN (Ch 15)

**AIDA:** Attention → Interest → Desire → Action

**REAN (adds post-purchase):** Reach → Engage → Activate → Nurture

**When to use:** Discussing how to market a new product, why users aren't converting, or how to grow a user base.

---

## Marketing Mix: 4 Ps (Ch 15)

- **Product** — what you're offering
- **Price** — pricing strategy and model
- **Promotion** — advertising, PR, word of mouth, sales
- **Place** — distribution channels

**When to use:** Designing a go-to-market strategy, analyzing why a product isn't selling.

---

## SWOT Analysis (Ch 15)

| | Good | Bad |
|--|------|-----|
| **Internal** | Strengths | Weaknesses |
| **External** | Opportunities | Threats |

**When to use:** Should we pursue this opportunity? What's our competitive position?

---

## Five Cs — Situational Analysis (Ch 15)

- **Company** — culture, products, strategy, brand, strengths/weaknesses
- **Competitors** — direct, potential, substitutes
- **Customers** — demographics, needs, market size, channels
- **Collaborators** — suppliers, distributors, partners
- **Climate** — regulations, tech changes, economic environment, cultural trends

**When to use:** Should we launch this product? What's the environment around this decision?

---

## Porter's 5 Forces (Ch 15)

1. Rivalry Among Existing Competitors
2. Buyer Power
3. Supplier Power
4. Threat of Substitutes
5. Threat of New Entrants

**When to use:** Should we enter this market? What's the industry competitive landscape?

---

## Metrics Framework: AARRR (Pirate Metrics)

- **Acquisition** — how users find you
- **Activation** — first meaningful experience
- **Retention** — do they come back?
- **Referral** — do they bring others?
- **Revenue** — do you make money?

---

## Metric Drop Diagnosis Framework

1. Is the data itself correct? (tracking bug, sampling error)
2. Did something change in the product? (feature launch, A/B test)
3. Is it external? (competitor, seasonality, news)
4. Is it a specific segment? (new vs. old users, device type, geography)
5. What are the most likely root causes? (hypothesize and rank)

## Amazon's 14 Leadership Principles (Ch 6)
Key ones for PM interviews:
1. Customer Obsession
2. Ownership
3. Invent and Simplify
4. Are Right, A Lot
5. Learn and Be Curious
6. Hire and Develop the Best
7. Insist on the Highest Standards
8. Think Big
9. Bias for Action
10. Frugality
11. Earn Trust
12. Dive Deep
13. Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit
14. Deliver Results

**For interview prep:** Map your resume stories to 3–4 of these and have concrete examples ready.
